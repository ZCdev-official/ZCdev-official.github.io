"You should not give up unless you are forced to."

Elon Musk

Every story has its beginning.

The first "real" programming that I did was on a platform called Roblox. When I first joined the platform, I was just having fun playing games. Then, I realized that I could make my own games to play on Roblox. The first game that I created actually involved no coding at all. And it sucked. As I grew more interested, I began exploring the documentation to learn more about coding for Roblox. As my experience with coding grew, the quality of my games improved significantly. However, I could never finish any of my projects on Roblox because I did not have the motivation to. On Roblox, the games that I made never got popular. As a result, I moved on to web development.

“It’s the job that’s never started that takes the longest to finish.”

J.R.R. Tolkien

Web development was scary at first. It was all so different from the coding language that I learned for Roblox (Lua). But once I got into, it was just a breeze. Everything felt so much easier, which led to boredom. As a result, I began to focus more on web design than web development, which kick-started my love of design. Once I started to focus on the design, I was faced with a new challenge. Coding websites was easy, but making them look good was hard. After diving deep into web development, I started to look at how to do graphic design.

Seeing things in a new light.

Graphic design started as a hobby. But after only a few days, I really got into it. At the time I was back into Roblox, which was the perfect platform for me to experiment with graphic design. I made images and logos for my games, and games for other people. I ended up eventually selling my services for a virtual currency on Roblox. However, I still yearned for something that would challenge me more. After a year or so, I found exactly what I was looking for.

Returning to old habits.

After years of searching, I finally found something that appealed to my love of design and my yearning for a coding challenge. Unity is a coding engine used for making games for various kinds of platforms. I had heard of it before, but I never really thought that I had the experience to use it. However, I came to realize that this was exactly the challenge that I was looking for. Developing on unity required knowing how to design and knowing how to code in C# or JavaScript. Although I had no experience in C# or Java, I was determined to learn these new coding languages. After a few months, I had made an app (Jump! - Infinite) and published it to the iOS app store. And this time, I actually had an audience who could play what I had created. Because so many of my friends own an Apple device, many of them downloaded my game. I was very satisfied and ready for more.
